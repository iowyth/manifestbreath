About

I am a performance artist and PhD student whose practice moves between dance, voice, illustration, and writing, engages across disciplines to examine participatory culture through a métis-crip-queer lens.

My work focuses on building horizontal power relations through community-based praxis, integrating creative expression with social justice and the utopic visioning of radical social alternatives.

I hold a BFA in Intermedia & Cyberarts from Concordia University, an MEd from Lakehead University, and am currently pursuing a PhD in Communication and Culture at Toronto Metropolitan University. I serve as Community Steward for the SORCE Collective.

Raised a Métis person within white settler culture, I seek the seeds of an Indigenous way of being within the texts of my life, looking for the echoes of a hidden, grounded, ecological humanity.

Portfolio

My practice draws from Butoh, ritual performance, and improvisational dance—seeking liminal spaces where bodies become thresholds. Informed by occult philosophy and embodied spirituality, I create transformative experiences that bridge personal narrative and collective catharsis. I have performed at venues including Artscape Gibraltar Point, The Garrison, Unit 2, and Wavelength Music Festival in Toronto.
